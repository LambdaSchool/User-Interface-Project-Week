Law, Order, Justice In The Wild West

Summary

Set in the Old West, this story follows a deputy and his quest to find the truth behind a mystery in the Colorado Territories. He will question witnesses and discover clues in an effort to uncover who is guilty, what weapon was used, and where the crime took place.

The Warrant

A warrant must be written up. It consists of eight individual characteristics, the weapon, and the place of the crime. For the warrant to be valid, the deputy must find two lawmen to sign the case. Once signed, a judge must give its official seal of approval.

THE SUSPECT

Witness testimonies and clues will give pieces of the puzzle to who the criminal is. The deputy must recognize the witness's own description, because they could be the suspect. Everything a witness says matters.

If not careful, the deputy can write down the wrong characteristics. When this happens, the wrong person could be charged or the guilty individual will be freed due to insufficient proof. Additionally, The criminal will get away if the deputy takes too long.

THE WEAPON

Witness testimonies and clues will help determine what weapon was used. Weapons include items and tools from every major location. The deputy must consider every aspect of the weapon described by the witnesses.

If not careful, the deputy can write down the wrong weapon. When this happens, the guilty individual will get away due to the lack of this critical piece of evidence. Additionally, The criminal will get away if the deputy takes too long.

THE PLACE

Witness testimonies and clues will make it clear where the crime took place. The deputy must consider both the broad location along with the specific place when witnesses give clues. Find the key witness to discover the detail that will give the specific place where it all unfolded.

If not careful, the deputy can write down the wrong location. When this happens, the guilty individual will be acquitted because the connection between individual and location was never made. Additionally, The criminal will get away if the deputy takes too long.
